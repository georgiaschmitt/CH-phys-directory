About Cluster Headache

also known as Horton's syndrome

Cluster headache is one of a group of headaches known as trigeminal autonomic cephalalgias (TACs). It is a primary headache—a headache in its own right, not caused by or a symptom of another illness—that causes severe, debilitating pain. It is generally considered to be the most painful medical condition known to mankind, described as worse than childbirth by most childbearing cluster patients.

The pain is usually focused around the eye socket/temple, sometimes spreading to other parts of the head like the ear, jaw, forehead and neck. Pain occurs usually on one side of the head, although it can switch sides between attacks. Attacks can be episodic, occurring on a daily basis over a period of weeks or months (a cluster) and then stop as suddenly as they started and go away for a period of weeks, months or even years, but later return. Attacks can also be chronic, with no or very short remission periods. At present, cluster headache is a lifelong, incurable condition. [OUCHuk]
