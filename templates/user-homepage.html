About Cluster Headache

also known as Horton's syndrome

Cluster headache [CH] is one of a group of headaches known as trigeminal autonomic cephalalgias [TACs]. It is a primary headache [a headache in its own right, not caused by or a symptom of another illness] that causes severe, debilitating pain. It is generally considered to be the most painful medical condition known to mankind and most female sufferers describe the pain as being worse than childbirth. The pain is usually focused around the eye socket/temple, sometimes spreading to other parts of the head like the ear, jaw, forehead and neck. The pain is usually on one side of the head, although it can switch sides between attacks, and rarely can change sides during an attack. At present it is a lifelong incurable condition.
